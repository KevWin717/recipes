Pancake

Description

A pancake is a flat, round breakfast dish made from a batter of flour, eggs, milk, and a leavening agent, cooked on a griddle or skillet. Light and fluffy, pancakes are often served stacked and topped with butter, syrup, fruits, or other sweet and savory additions, making them a versatile and beloved comfort food.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup milk (or any milk alternative)
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter (or oil for non-dairy)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare Dry Ingredients:
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine 1 cup of flour, 2 tablespoons of sugar, 1 teaspoon of baking powder, 1/2 teaspoon of baking soda, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt. Mix well.
  2. Prepare Wet Ingredients:
    • In a separate bowl, whisk together 3/4 cup milk, 1 egg, 2 tablespoons melted butter (or oil), and 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional).
  3. Combine the Mixtures:
    • Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Stir gently until just combined. (It’s okay if the batter has a few lumps.)
  4. Preheat the Pan:
    • Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lightly grease with butter or oil.
  5. Cook the Pancakes:
    • Scoop about 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake.
    • Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges look set (about 2-3 minutes).
  6. Flip and Finish Cooking:
    • Carefully flip the pancake and cook the other side until golden brown (about 1-2 minutes).
  7. Serve Warm:
    • Stack the pancakes and serve with your favorite toppings: syrup, butter, fruits, whipped cream, or nuts.
